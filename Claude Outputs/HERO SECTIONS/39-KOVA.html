Our Philosophy

We design against
the forgettable.

KOVA was founded on a single premise: the best interiors are not decorated — they are structured. We begin with proportion, light, and material truth, then layer in texture, warmth, and detail until the room has an unmistakable character.

We work slowly, because permanence requires it. We refuse commissions we cannot execute without compromise. We measure success by how a space feels after five years, not five minutes.

Structural First

Proportion and light before any material decision

Material Honesty

Every surface is exactly what it appears to be

Slow Practice

We limit our active projects to maintain quality

Enduring Detail

Joinery, hardware, and edge details worth inheriting

How We Work

The KOVA
method

Every project follows our structured approach — built over sixteen years of practice to protect creative quality while keeping you informed at every turn.

Start a Conversation
01

Discovery & Brief

We meet to understand how you live, what you value, what you want to feel in the space — not just what you want it to look like. The brief is the foundation; we spend time getting it right.

Week 1–2
02

Concept Development

Spatial planning, material direction, and a visual narrative — presented as a cohesive concept, not a mood board of options. We bring a point of view, then refine it with your input.

Week 3–6
03

Design Development

Technical drawings, 3D visualisations, bespoke furniture specifications, material samples, and FF&E schedules. The design is resolved in full before any contractor is engaged.

Month 2–4
04

Procurement & Contracting

We manage supplier relationships, negotiate on your behalf, review contractor quotes, and sequence orders to avoid delays. Every purchase is documented and tracked against the agreed schedule.

Month 3–5
05

Installation & Completion

On-site coordination, snagging, final dressing, and a formal handover. We do not consider a project complete until every detail is resolved — including the ones that were not in the original scope.

Month 5–9
Project Estimator
