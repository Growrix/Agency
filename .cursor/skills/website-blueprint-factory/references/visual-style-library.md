# Visual Style Library

The `bp-04-visual-architecture-director` must choose **exactly ONE primary style** per project and justify it against the industry (Stage 01) and positioning (Stage 03). A secondary "accent" style may be borrowed sparingly, but the primary defines the visual DNA.

**Never default to "Modern SaaS / generic dashboard blue."** If a clean SaaS look is chosen, it must be argued for, not defaulted to.

Each style below lists: **Feel**, **Signature traits**, **Color/type tendency**, **Best for**, **Avoid for**, **Pairs well with** (grid/motion hints).

---

## 1. Editorial Luxury
- **Feel:** A high-end magazine or fashion book. Confident, spacious, curated.
- **Signature traits:** Oversized serif headlines, generous whitespace, asymmetric text/image blocks, thin rules, restrained palette, large editorial imagery.
- **Color/type:** Ink black + paper white + one muted accent; serif display + clean sans body.
- **Best for:** Luxury real estate, fashion, hospitality, architecture, premium services, wineries.
- **Avoid for:** High-density data products, budget/value brands, fast-conversion lead-gen.
- **Pairs with:** Editorial grid, Cinematic or Architectural motion.

## 2. Modern Minimalism
- **Feel:** Quiet confidence. Everything earns its place.
- **Signature traits:** Lots of negative space, few colors, strong type hierarchy, subtle dividers, content-first.
- **Color/type:** Near-monochrome + single accent; geometric sans.
- **Best for:** Design studios, B2B SaaS that wants to feel premium, consultancies, portfolios.
- **Avoid for:** Brands needing high energy or playful warmth.
- **Pairs with:** Swiss grid, Apple Fluid motion.

## 3. Glassmorphism
- **Feel:** Frosted glass floating over color. Light, modern, layered.
- **Signature traits:** Translucent blurred panels, soft borders, vivid background gradients/blobs behind glass cards.
- **Color/type:** Saturated gradient backgrounds, white/translucent surfaces; rounded sans.
- **Best for:** Fintech, crypto, modern mobile apps, futuristic dashboards.
- **Avoid for:** Accessibility-sensitive contexts unless contrast is carefully managed; print-like brands.
- **Pairs with:** Bento grid, Elastic/Layered motion.

## 4. Neumorphism
- **Feel:** Soft, tactile, "pressed into the surface."
- **Signature traits:** Soft inner/outer shadows, low contrast, monochrome surfaces, pill controls.
- **Color/type:** Single base hue with light/dark shadow pairs; soft rounded sans.
- **Best for:** Calm utility apps, audio/IoT product sites, wellness tech.
- **Avoid for:** Anything requiring strong contrast or fast scanning; accessibility-critical sites (use with care).
- **Pairs with:** Dashboard grid, Material/Physics motion.

## 5. Brutalism
- **Feel:** Raw, loud, anti-corporate, confident.
- **Signature traits:** Exposed structure, heavy borders, mono fonts, harsh contrast, intentional "ugly," oversized type.
- **Color/type:** High-contrast, often black/white + one shock color; monospace or grotesque sans.
- **Best for:** Creative agencies, music, fashion drops, art, bold DTC brands.
- **Avoid for:** Trust-sensitive industries (healthcare, finance, legal).
- **Pairs with:** Asymmetrical/Split grid, Magnetic/Elastic motion.

## 6. Swiss Design
- **Feel:** Objective, rational, grid-perfect International Typographic Style.
- **Signature traits:** Strict baseline grid, Helvetica-like type, flush-left, mathematical spacing, minimal ornament.
- **Color/type:** Black/white/red classic or restrained; neo-grotesque sans.
- **Best for:** Editorial platforms, design tools, museums, B2B clarity.
- **Avoid for:** Warm lifestyle or playful consumer brands.
- **Pairs with:** Swiss grid, Architectural motion.

## 7. Futuristic Tech
- **Feel:** Next-gen, sci-fi adjacent, high-energy.
- **Signature traits:** Dark base, neon/electric accents, glow, HUD elements, animated gradients, monospace accents.
- **Color/type:** Dark + electric cyan/violet; geometric sans + mono.
- **Best for:** AI products, developer tools, gaming, deep tech, web3.
- **Avoid for:** Local services, traditional/heritage brands.
- **Pairs with:** Bento/Dashboard grid, Layered/Physics motion.

## 8. Industrial Modern
- **Feel:** Engineered, robust, no-nonsense, "built to spec."
- **Signature traits:** Strong grids, technical labels, blueprint lines, monospace data, muted metallics, photography of work/process.
- **Color/type:** Steel/graphite + safety accent; condensed sans + mono.
- **Best for:** Construction, manufacturing, logistics, trades, engineering, automotive.
- **Avoid for:** Soft lifestyle, beauty, children's brands.
- **Pairs with:** Swiss/Dashboard grid, Architectural motion.

## 9. Organic Nature
- **Feel:** Earthy, calm, human, sustainable.
- **Signature traits:** Soft organic shapes, natural palette, textured imagery, hand-touched details, generous curves.
- **Color/type:** Greens/clays/sand; humanist serif or soft sans.
- **Best for:** Wellness, sustainability, food/agriculture, skincare, landscaping, eco brands.
- **Avoid for:** High-tech, hard-edged enterprise.
- **Pairs with:** Editorial/Masonry grid, Organic motion.

## 10. Hospitality Luxury
- **Feel:** Warm, indulgent, sensory, "you are a welcomed guest."
- **Signature traits:** Full-bleed imagery, warm neutrals, gold accents, elegant serifs, slow reveals, ambient detail.
- **Color/type:** Warm cream/charcoal + brass; refined serif display.
- **Best for:** Hotels, resorts, fine dining, spas, premium travel, event venues.
- **Avoid for:** Budget, utilitarian, or data-heavy products.
- **Pairs with:** Editorial grid, Cinematic motion.

## 11. Dark Luxury
- **Feel:** Exclusive, premium, mysterious, after-dark.
- **Signature traits:** Deep black/charcoal base, metallic accents (gold/champagne), spotlight contrast, refined type, restrained motion.
- **Color/type:** Near-black + gold/platinum; high-contrast serif or refined sans.
- **Best for:** Luxury auto, jewelry, premium spirits, private clubs, high-end agencies.
- **Avoid for:** Friendly mass-market, kids, budget.
- **Pairs with:** Editorial/Split grid, Cinematic/Architectural motion.

## 12. Retro Modern
- **Feel:** Nostalgic but current — vintage warmth with modern polish.
- **Signature traits:** Period palettes (70s/80s/90s), grain/texture, retro type, badges, playful shapes used tastefully.
- **Color/type:** Warm vintage palette; expressive display + clean body.
- **Best for:** Coffee/craft brands, music, lifestyle DTC, creative studios, hospitality with character.
- **Avoid for:** Enterprise, medical, finance.
- **Pairs with:** Asymmetrical/Masonry grid, Elastic/Organic motion.

## 13. Bento Interface
- **Feel:** Modular, organized, "everything in its tile" (Apple keynote style).
- **Signature traits:** Grid of varied-size rounded cards, each a self-contained feature/stat/visual, balanced composition.
- **Color/type:** Clean neutrals + accent per tile; modern sans.
- **Best for:** Product/feature showcases, SaaS, hardware, portfolios, dashboards-as-marketing.
- **Avoid for:** Long-form storytelling-first narratives.
- **Pairs with:** Bento grid, Apple Fluid/Layered motion.

## 14. Cyberpunk
- **Feel:** Neon dystopia, high-intensity, rebellious.
- **Signature traits:** Neon on near-black, glitch effects, scanlines, bold gradients, dense layered UI, aggressive type.
- **Color/type:** Black + magenta/cyan/lime neon; techno display + mono.
- **Best for:** Gaming, streetwear, music, web3, edgy entertainment.
- **Avoid for:** Trust-critical or calm/wellness brands.
- **Pairs with:** Dashboard/Asymmetrical grid, Physics/Magnetic motion.

## 15. Monochromatic Minimal
- **Feel:** One color, infinite restraint. Sophisticated and focused.
- **Signature traits:** Single hue across tints/shades, near-zero decoration, type-led, precise spacing.
- **Color/type:** One color family + neutral; refined sans.
- **Best for:** Design portfolios, premium consultancies, art, photography, minimalist DTC.
- **Avoid for:** Brands needing energy variety or multi-category navigation.
- **Pairs with:** Swiss/Editorial grid, Apple Fluid motion.

## 16. Skeuomorphic Premium
- **Feel:** Realistic materials and depth — leather, metal, glass, paper — done tastefully.
- **Signature traits:** Realistic textures, depth, material shadows, tactile controls, photographic realism.
- **Color/type:** Material-derived palettes; classic type.
- **Best for:** Watch/jewelry, instruments, premium hardware, craftsmanship brands.
- **Avoid for:** Fast minimalist SaaS, performance-critical mobile.
- **Pairs with:** Split/Editorial grid, Physics/Cinematic motion.

## 17. Data-Centric Design
- **Feel:** Information is the hero. Clear, scannable, trustworthy.
- **Signature traits:** Charts, tables, KPI cards, dense but legible layouts, strong hierarchy, functional color coding.
- **Color/type:** Neutral base + semantic data colors; legible sans + tabular numerals.
- **Best for:** Analytics, fintech, B2B platforms, dashboards, marketplaces, comparison tools.
- **Avoid for:** Emotion-led lifestyle/luxury storytelling.
- **Pairs with:** Dashboard/Swiss grid, Material/Apple Fluid motion.

---

## Selection guidance for `bp-04`
1. Eliminate styles that clash with industry trust factors (Stage 01) — e.g. brutalism/cyberpunk for healthcare.
2. From survivors, pick the one that best expresses the **positioning differentiation** (Stage 03), not the industry cliché (Stage 02).
3. Cross-check the prior projects' styles in `_uniqueness-ledger.md` — avoid reusing the same style for the same/adjacent industry.
4. Justify in `04-visual-architecture.md`: why this style, why not the obvious one, and what it signals to the buyer.
