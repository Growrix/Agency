Tree Knowledge Center

Questions, answered

When should a tree be removed?
Removal is warranted when a tree is dead, structurally unsound, diseased beyond recovery, or poses an unacceptable risk to people or property. A certified arborist evaluates decay, lean, root health and target zones before recommending removal — preservation is always the first option we explore.
How do I know if a tree is dangerous?
Warning signs include large dead branches, cracks in the trunk or major limbs, fungal growth at the base, a sudden or pronounced lean, lifting soil around the roots, and hollow sounds when tapped. Try our Tree Risk Assessment Scanner for preliminary guidance, then book an on-site inspection.
Can diseased trees be saved?
Often, yes — especially when caught early. Many diseases and pest infestations respond well to targeted treatment, pruning of affected areas, soil management and improved care. Our arborists diagnose the specific issue and provide a treatment plan whenever recovery is realistic.
What happens after storm damage?
First, we secure the area and remove immediate hazards 24/7. Then we assess remaining trees for hidden damage, document everything for insurance claims, and create a recovery plan. We support your insurance process with detailed reports and photographs.
Do I need permits for tree work?
It depends on your municipality, tree size and species — many areas protect heritage or street trees. We handle permit research and applications as part of our consultation service, so you stay compliant and avoid fines.
What exactly is an arborist?
An arborist is a trained, certified professional who specializes in the cultivation, management and study of trees. ISA Certified Arborists pass rigorous exams and maintain ongoing education — they understand tree biology, not just tree cutting, which is why they can preserve assets others would simply remove.

Tree Knowledge Center

Questions, answered
by certified arborists.

The most common questions property owners ask us — about safety, removal, disease, permits and what to do after a storm.

When should a tree be removed?
Removal is warranted when a tree is dead, structurally unsound beyond repair, leaning dangerously toward a target, or so diseased that it can't be saved. But removal is always our last resort — many trees that look hazardous can be preserved with cabling, pruning or treatment. An on-site assessment determines the safest path.
How do I know if a tree is dangerous?
Warning signs include a sudden lean, cracks in the trunk, fungal growth at the base, large dead branches, heaving soil around the roots, and cavities or hollows. Any of these near a home, driveway or power line warrants a professional inspection. Our free Tree Risk Scanner gives you a quick preliminary read.
Can diseased trees be saved?
Very often, yes — especially when caught early. Many fungal infections, pest infestations and nutrient deficiencies respond well to targeted treatment, soil care and structural pruning. The key is accurate diagnosis. We assess the type and extent of disease, then recommend treatment when recovery is realistic and removal only when it isn't.
What happens after storm damage?
First, ensure everyone is safe and stay clear of downed lines. Then call our 24/7 emergency line. Our crews stabilize immediate hazards, perform safe removal where needed, and assess surrounding trees for secondary failure risk. We also provide documentation to support insurance claims and a full cleanup.
Do I need permits to remove a tree?
It depends on your municipality, tree size and species — many cities protect heritage and street trees. We handle permitting research and applications as part of our service, and our arborist reports satisfy most city requirements. We'll tell you exactly what's needed before any work begins.
What is an arborist?
An arborist is a trained, certified professional who specializes in the care of individual trees. ISA Certified Arborists pass rigorous exams in tree biology, diagnosis, pruning, safety and risk assessment. Unlike general landscapers, arborists understand trees as living systems — which is why our recommendations prioritize long-term health and preservation.
Are you licensed and insured?
Fully. Heartwood Arbor Co. carries $2M in liability coverage plus workers' compensation, and every crew works to ANSI A300 and Z133 safety standards. We're happy to provide certificates of insurance on request — never hire an uninsured tree company.
