FAQ

Common questions about solar in Australia

How much do solar panels cost in Australia in 2026?

A quality 6.6 kW residential system typically ranges from $5,500–$9,500 AUD after STC rebates, depending on panel tier, inverter brand, roof complexity and state.

What are STCs and how do they reduce my price?

Small-scale Technology Certificates (STCs) are created when eligible solar is installed. Your retailer claims them upfront as a point-of-sale discount based on system size, postcode zone factor and deeming period under the RET.

Do I need CEC accreditation?

Yes — only Clean Energy Council (CEC) accredited designers and installers can claim STCs and connect systems under Australian standards. Sunterra is fully CEC accredited.

How long does installation take?

Most residential installs complete in 1–2 days after DNSP approval. From contract to switch-on, allow 4–8 weeks for approvals and inspection.

Will solar work during a blackout?

Standard grid-tied systems shut down for safety. With a battery and backup gateway, essential or whole-home circuits can stay powered.
